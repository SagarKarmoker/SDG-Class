About sdgacademy

SDG Classroom is an online education initiative that offers free, high-quality courses on sustainable development and the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). It provides learning materials designed by experts and leaders in sustainability, covering topics like climate action, poverty reduction, and global health. The platform aims to empower individuals and organizations to contribute to achieving the SDGs by 2030. Courses are accessible to learners worldwide, catering to both beginners and professionals.

Introduction to Weather and Climate

Objective:

Introduce students to the difference between weather and climate, focusing on how both impact daily life and long-term environmental conditions.

Materials Needed:

  • Whiteboard/Projector
  • Weather data charts
  • Student worksheets on weather and climate
  • QR code for weather elements (from the textbook)

Lesson Plan:

  1. Introduction (10 minutes):
    • Discuss the scenario from the book (coastal town with changing weather) to introduce the difference between weather and climate.
    • Define weather and climate:
      • Weather: short-term atmospheric conditions.
      • Climate: long-term patterns in a particular area.
  2. Discussion and Activity (15 minutes):
    • Compare weather data from a particular day to historical climate data. Ask students to discuss how weather varies while climate stays consistent over time.
    • Have students read section 7.1.1 (Weather) and section 7.1.2 (Climate) from the book and fill out a worksheet comparing different weather elements (e.g., temperature, wind, humidity).
    • Use the QR code to explore the elements of weather in more detail.
  3. Homework:

    Ask students to observe the weather over the next few days and predict the forecast based on weather reports.

Climate Change and Its Causes

Objective:

Introduce students to climate change, focusing on both natural and human-induced causes.

Materials Needed:

  • Textbook (section 7.2 Climate Change).
  • Visual aids on greenhouse gases.
  • Poster paper for group activity.

Lesson Plan:

  1. Introduction (10 minutes):
    • Define climate change and discuss its impact on weather patterns, including rising temperatures, melting ice caps, and increased storms.
    • Explain both natural causes (volcanic eruptions, solar radiation) and human-induced causes (deforestation, greenhouse gas emissions).
  2. Group Activity (20 minutes):
    • Divide students into groups and have them create posters illustrating the causes of climate change.
    • Each group presents their findings, focusing on either human or natural causes of climate change.
  3. Wrap-Up and Homework:
    • Ask students to read section 7.2.1 (The Greenhouse Effect) and write a summary on how it contributes to global warming.

The Greenhouse Effect and SDG 13

Objective:

Explore the greenhouse effect in depth and link it to SDG 13 (Climate Action).

Materials Needed:

  • Greenhouse effect simulation (online or physical demonstration).
  • Textbook (section 7.2.1 The Greenhouse Effect).
  • SDG 13 poster.

Lesson Plan:

  1. Introduction (10 minutes):
    • Use a simulation to demonstrate how greenhouse gases trap heat in the Earth's atmosphere, similar to a greenhouse.
    • Define greenhouse gases (carbon dioxide, methane, etc.) and discuss their sources.
  2. Discussion (15 minutes):
    • Discuss how human activities like fossil fuel burning and deforestation increase greenhouse gases.
    • Link this to SDG 13, emphasizing the need for urgent climate action to reduce emissions and slow global warming.
  3. Class Activity (15 minutes):
    • Students work in pairs to brainstorm ways they can contribute to climate action in their community (e.g., tree planting, reducing plastic use).
    • Share ideas as a class and discuss how each action can help achieve SDG 13.

Acid Rain, Ozone Layer Depletion, and SDG 15

Objective:

Teach students about the effects of acid rain and ozone depletion on life and how they connect to SDG 15 (Life on Land).

Materials Needed:

  • Visual aids on acid rain and ozone depletion.
  • Textbook (sections 7.2.2 and 7.2.3).
  • SDG 15 poster.

Lesson Plan:

  1. Introduction (10 minutes):
    • Define acid rain and explain how sulfur dioxide and nitrogen oxides mix with rainwater to form it.
    • Show examples of the effects of acid rain on ecosystems, buildings, and aquatic life.
  2. Activity (20 minutes):
    • Discuss ozone layer depletion and its role in blocking harmful UV rays. Explain the harmful effects of increased UV exposure on humans, plants, and animals.
    • Connect this to SDG 15, focusing on how protecting ecosystems and reducing pollution help sustain life on land.
  3. Interactive Quiz (10 minutes):
    • Use an online quiz to test students’ understanding of acid rain and the ozone layer.

Climate Change Solutions and Project Work

Objective:

Encourage students to propose real-world solutions to combat climate change and achieve SDGs 13 and 15.

Materials Needed:

  • Project guidelines for student presentations
  • Computer for research

Lesson Plan:

  1. Introduction (5 minutes):
    • Recap the lessons on climate change, the greenhouse effect, acid rain, and the ozone layer. Discuss the role of global agreements like the Montreal Protocol.
  2. Group Project (30 minutes):
    • Students will work in groups to research a climate change problem and propose solutions (e.g., reducing greenhouse gases, protecting biodiversity).
    • Each group will prepare a short presentation explaining how their solution contributes to SDG 13 or 15.
  3. Wrap-Up (5 minutes):

    Discuss the importance of taking individual and collective action to fight climate change.

Assessment:

  • Class participation in discussions and activities.
  • Group project presentations on climate solutions.
  • Homework and quizzes testing knowledge of climate change, the greenhouse effect, acid rain, and SDGs.

Extensions:

  • Take students on a field trip to a local weather station or environmental research center.
  • Start a climate action project in school (e.g., a tree-planting drive).
